Title: Lightweight CNN-Aided Timing Synchronization for OFDM Systems Using Cascaded Mode

Authors: J. Kim, L. Chen, R. Patel

Abstract:
Accurate timing synchronization is a fundamental requirement in orthogonal frequency-division
multiplexing (OFDM) systems. Classical correlation-based methods suffer under severe
multipath and low signal-to-noise ratio conditions. In this paper, we propose a lightweight
convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture exploiting a cascaded mode for improved
timing offset estimation. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms
state-of-the-art baselines by up to 3.2 dB at low SNR.

---

Introduction:
Orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) has become the dominant waveform in
modern broadband wireless communications, including LTE and 5G NR standards [1]. A
critical challenge in OFDM receivers is accurate timing synchronization: an incorrect
timing offset causes inter-symbol interference and degrades system throughput significantly.

The problem of timing synchronization was first proposed in the seminal work of Schmidl
and Cox [2], who introduced a correlation-based method using repeated pilot symbols. This
classical approach, while elegant, degrades under severe multipath fading channels. Van de
Beek et al. [3] later proposed a maximum-likelihood estimator for joint timing and frequency
offset recovery, extending the pioneering work of [2] to cyclic-prefix-based detection.

Recent advances in deep learning have motivated the application of neural networks to
physical-layer tasks [4]. Prior work by Wang et al. [5] applied deep neural networks to
channel estimation, while previous studies by Liu et al. [6] explored recurrent networks
for timing correction in OFDM systems. These results suggest that data-driven methods can
complement or replace conventional signal processing blocks.

In this paper, we revisit the timing synchronization problem and propose a CNN-based
approach operating in a novel cascaded mode. Unlike prior work, our model is designed to
be lightweight and suitable for embedded deployment.

---

Related Work:
Timing synchronization in OFDM systems has a rich literature. The foundational work of
Schmidl and Cox [2] remains a standard reference, and subsequent extensions addressed
fractional offset estimation [3] and robustness under frequency-selective fading [7].

Previous studies on machine-learning-based synchronization focused primarily on frequency
offset estimation [5]. The prior work of Park et al. [8] introduced a convolutional
feature extractor for timing detection, which we build upon in this paper. Yang et al. [9]
demonstrated that cascaded architectures can reduce model complexity while preserving
accuracy, an observation central to our design.

---

System Model:
We consider a standard OFDM system with N subcarriers and cyclic prefix length L_cp.
The received baseband signal after passing through a multipath channel is denoted r[n].
Timing synchronization aims to estimate the integer timing offset theta such that the
correct FFT window is applied.

---

Proposed Method:
Our CNN operates on a short sliding window of received samples. The network consists of
two convolutional stages (cascaded mode) followed by a fully connected layer. The first
stage extracts coarse timing features; the second stage refines the estimate. This
cascaded design reduces parameter count compared to a single deep network [9].

---

Experiment:
We evaluate the proposed method against four baselines on the ITU Vehicular-A channel
model [10] using the standard COST 207 dataset [11].

We compare with the Schmidl-Cox detector [2] as the primary classical baseline. We also
compare with the ML-based timing method of Park et al. [8] and the recurrent approach
of Liu et al. [6], which serve as direct deep-learning baselines.

The proposed method is additionally compared against the state-of-the-art transformer-based
synchronizer of Zhao et al. [12], which currently achieves the best published results on
this benchmark. Our method outperforms [12] by 2.1 dB at SNR = 0 dB.

Evaluation follows the protocol of [10] using root-mean-square error (RMSE) of the
timing offset as the primary metric [13]. Secondary metrics include probability of
correct synchronization P_cs and computational complexity in FLOPs.

---

Results:
The proposed cascaded CNN achieves the lowest RMSE across all tested SNR values.
At SNR = -5 dB, our method reduces RMSE by 34% compared against the classical
Schmidl-Cox baseline [2] and by 18% compared against the deep-learning baseline [8].
State-of-the-art comparison confirms that our lightweight model matches [12] in accuracy
while using 6x fewer parameters.

---

Discussion:
These results support the conclusion that cascaded convolutional processing is an effective
inductive bias for timing synchronization. Supporting evidence from [4] and [9] suggests
that the cascaded structure implicitly learns a coarse-to-fine estimation hierarchy.
